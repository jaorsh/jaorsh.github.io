About Me

Beginning to spin a yarn about me, I find I get easily overwhelmed by the BIG questions. Like, why am I who I am what I am who am I? To be honest, I don't really understand. It's just too easy to say:

Name Josh Hare.
Description Geek.
LUVS Exploring, travelling, banter, memories, the internet, the ocean, friends, family, live music, making music, secret music, haXXing, all nighters, smashing out good code, no reception, reception.
H8Z Over analysing stuff, hypocrisy, spelling hypocrisy, social class, over poaching eggs and too much marmite, 20 minute jam from a band you are trying to like but then you see them live and they just jam for 20 minutes one dude's got a violin bow scraping his guitar strings it's like cmon play a real song drop the violin guy.

But a list doesn't say much about me. It even looks like I have more hates then loves. That's simply not true, lists can be decieving. So maybe it's better to just look for a context. A context to encompass a thought about something a tad real about me.

Well, currently I want to be surrounded by colour - looking out of my window into a bleak yet beautiful Hells Kitchen. This place convinced me to throw together something vibrant - welcome to my website. In search of this vibrancy, in my everlong battle finding colour in this neon city, I often close my eyes at the end of the day and think back to the few times I've witnessed a sore thumb stuck right out. One of those times you feel like an outsider, unworthy of whatever beauty you've stumbled upon - a landscape, a person, a sound.

Growing up in New Zealand means being spoilt by imagery and audio from a vast array of natures offerings. Spoilt rotten. See I love tech, but it's one thing blasting a rainforest shower through headphones before sleeping, and another entirely to open my minds eye, to stretch across Milford Sound. So I do that. I think most people do that, go their own places, but it feels unique. It feels where I've come from, where I've gone to and the juicy bits in between. It feels like something about me.
