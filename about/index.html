About Me

Beginning to spin a yarn about me, I find I get easily overwhelmed by the BIG questions. Like, why am I who I am what I am who am I? To be honest, I don't really understand. It's just too easy to say:

Name Josh Hare.
Description Geek.
LUVS Exploring, travelling, banter, memories, the internet, the ocean, friends, family, live music, making music, secret music, a decent workflow, smashing out good code, no reception, reception.
H8Z Crashing, Over analysing, hypocrisy, spelling hypocrisy, over poaching eggs and too much marmite, 20 minute jams from a band you think you might love but instead you see them live and they just jam for 20 minutes - one dude's got a violin bow scraping his guitar strings and there's a bitter feeling in the crowd air because they aren't The Grateful Dead and no one has heard a song yet. That's the worst!

But a list doesn't say much about me. It even looks like I have more hates then loves. That's simply not true - lists can be decieving. So maybe it's better to just look for a context. A context to encompass a thought about something a tad real about me.

Well, currently I want to be surrounded by colour - looking out of my window into a bleak yet beautiful Hells Kitchen. This place convinced me to throw together something vibrant - welcome to my website. In search of this vibrancy, in my everlong battle finding colour in this neon city, I often close my eyes at the end of the day and think back to the few times I've witnessed a sore thumb stuck right out. One of those times you feel like an outsider, unworthy of whatever beauty you've stumbled upon - a landscape, a person, a sound.

Growing up in New Zealand means being spoilt by imagery and audio from a vast array of natures offerings. Spoilt rotten. See I love tech, but it's one thing blasting a rainforest shower through headphones before sleeping, and another entirely to open my minds eye, to stretch across Milford Sound. So I do that. I think most people do that, go their own places, but it feels unique. It feels where I've come from, where I've gone to and the juicy bits in between. It feels like something about me.

Build process

Webpack

We use webpack as a JS builder and dependency retriever reading from `package.json`. It provides a node like front end modular solution for requiring javascript libraries, and turns a spaghetti of dependencies and lose JS files into easy to manage static assets.

Gulp

Gulp is used to automate our build process. It generates our javascript we distribute in `dist/app.min.js`. As the filename suggests, the outputted javascript file has all of the scripts needed to run the app. These are minified and concatenated together automatically with gulp. The gulp config leverages `webpack` to use a node-like modular javascript require model, and can be found at `gulpfile.js`.

Jekyll

This webapp is served with Jekyll through GitHub pages. Jekyll is simple to use, either through GitHub or on a local server, you can specify `_templates` and `_includes` to provide structural markup to your static html pages. We are only using includes - header and footer - in this project, as all pages use the same template. You can find the header.html and footer.html in the `_includes` folder.

Jekyll also can serve clean URL's by structuring index.html files within folders with the given name. http://jaorsh.github.io/about/index.html is http://jaorsh.github.io/about/ on the front end. In this webapp we are using History JS to retrieve our pages and populate clean urls, as requests are fired off with Ajax. Still, for manual navigation to say, /about, this feature of Jekyll is great!
